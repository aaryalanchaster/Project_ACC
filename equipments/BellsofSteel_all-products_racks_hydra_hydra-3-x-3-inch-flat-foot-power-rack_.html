Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack

The flat foot power rack — where classic design and rock-solid, no-bolt-down-needed stability team up with a chalky fist bump of swoleship.

So sturdy. So customizable. So very much worth the financial sacrifice for a never-have-to-upgrade power rack. You know what they say though… buy once, cry once.

Scroll down as I take you through the magical journey of building your perfect power rack!

FEATURES • True 3″ x 3″ uprights • 5/8″ holes • 11-gauge steel  • 1,000lb capacity • Black powder coat • Hardware included • Ships unassembled

Choose Your Height 1 Choose Your Height

The classic 84" is the most popular, since the average basement height is 96".

Choose the 90" if you've got the height and love a commanding look.

Short on height in the basement? Get yourself the 72" to fit like a glove.

*Keep in mind the flat foot adds 2-1⁄4" to the overall height of your rack.

Choose Your Crossmember Depth 2 Choose Your Crossmember Depth

30-inch is the tried and true choice, the goldilocks of space saving with enough room to walk your squat out.

Love driving your Cadillac down a 2 lane highway that had 1 lane removed with paint thinner? Then go with the decadent 43-inch for maximum room and comfort.

Is your space (and your wallet) toight like a tiger? Then much like the best beverage quantity, a 2-4 is the choice for you.

*When it comes to space planning, the depth of rack will depend on the size of flat foot support you select in section 11 at the bottom

Choose Your Pull-Up Bar 3 Choose Your Pull-Up Bar

Straight pull-up bar if you're a no-fuss, straight-to-business kinda person.

Double if you like the classic look and fat grip option, plus it's wide enough to go in the middle of your rack without needing a back cross-piece.

Multi-grip has the same pros as above, with the added utility of cross handles for even more pull-up variations.

Adjustable gives you a little extra variety if you want to move it around for different exercises, or if your training partner is a little on the short (or tall!) side.

Globe, so many activities... hopefully, you're into rock climbing! Can also be added on the side if you select 43-inches as your rack depth.

Choose Your Back Cross Piece 4 Choose Your Back Cross Piece

Choose the standard cross member (includes a little logo plate), or ritz it up with the deluxe logo plate cross member.

Just can't get enough pull-ups? Select another pull-up bar as your cross piece.

If you already selected the double or multi-grip bar in the "Choose Your Pull-up Bar" step, you can place it in the middle of your rack and skip out on adding an actual back cross-piece by clicking on the next step.

Logo Plate Crossmember is on backorder - shipping December 2nd.

Choose Your Safeties 5 Choose Your Safeties

Sorry, friend. The length of your safeties is auto-set to match the depth of your rack, but you DO get to choose the type!

Safety straps are more forgiving to dropped bars, but pin pipe safeties involve less guesswork (and profanity) when finding your ideal safeties' height.

Or, keep it simple by rocking a pair of trusty spotter arms instead which fit outside any depth and can go inside on the 43-inch depth and just squeeze in the 30 inch.

Spotter arms are on back order - shipping December 2nd.

Choose Your J-Cups 6 Choose Your J-Cups

Opt for the default tried-and-true Hydra j-cups with PVC protection on all sides and an engrained BOS logo.

Upgrade to the sandwich j-cups, a more robust option from the standard cups and a serious plus when using a squat safety bar.

Treat yourself to the unique and highly functional roller j-cups. For all the bench press lovers out there, this is a must.

Or splurge on yourself and buy a pair of each!

Add Storage (Optional) 7 Add Storage (Optional)

Storage — the least cool (but most important) part of your gym.

Don't make a rookie mistake and skip out on this! Your stub-free big toe will thank us later.

Add Hydra Attachments (Optional) 8 Add Hydra Attachments (Optional)

Time to make this beauty your own!

Jazz it up with a cornucopia of attachment options for blasting through plateaus, and magnifying your muscles.

Add Lat Pulldown (Optional) 9 Add Lat Pulldown (Optional)

Lat pulldowns, seated rows, bicep curls, tricep presses and whatever your heart desires. Add this quintessential attachment to your rack, aluminum pulleys included! Height is auto-selected based on your chosen upright height.

Note that this attachment is NOT compatible with the flat foot 43" back cross-member

Add Hardware (Optional) 10 Add Hardware (Optional)

All nuts, bolts, pins, and a small logo plate are included by default but you can add more here.

Wrenches and concrete anchors are not included, but can be purchased separately below.

Choose your Flat Foot Length 11 Choose your Flat Foot Length

The 56" length can be paired with the 30" or 24" cross member depth, while the 62" length can be paired with the 43", 30" or 24" depth.

Popular combos are 43" with a 62" flat foot for maximum roominess.
30" with the 56" flat foot for maximum stability and minimal space.
Or get a little weird with the 24" depth and 56" flat foot.

You can select the flat foot with a bottom cross member that connects the 2 feet for increased stability, or leave it out. Note that the bottom cross member is NOT compatible with the lat pulldown attachment.

    Benefits of the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack

    • STABLE FLAT-FOOT STYLE
      The 4 post Flat Foot power rack is the granddaddy of all rack configurations. If you want your rack to outlast heavy squats, handle big bench presses and withstand a set of chin-ups with 8 kids latching onto your waist, then this is the rack to get. This bad boy weighs a hefty +250 pounds and unlike the 4 Post With No Feet, its design is ultra-stable — so you don’t have to bolt it down before trying for that new chin-up PR. Oh, and did we mention you’ll fit in with the cool crowd with this rack? *wink wink*
    • COMPATIBLE WITH OTHER BRANDS
      If you’re a die-hard fan of the metric system, then listen up: the uprights of our Hydra racks measure true 3″ x 3″ which is EXACTLY 76.2mm x 76.2mm, instead of the typical 75mm x 75mm uprights of other brands’ imported racks. By matching the same tubing specs as many of our American competitors who make their racks on US soil *respectfully tips hat*, you can mix and match attachments freely without needing to stick to one brand — hello, open ecosystem! Just be sure to research thoroughly BEFORE you begin the mingling shenanigans, deal?
    • MODULAR DESIGN
      QUICK! Name three things in life you can’t live without. I’ll go first — pockets, sliced bread, and… a customizable power rack 😎 Choose a pre-made design like you see here or build your own piece-by-piece. You can always add separate components later when you’re ready to expand your unit with weight plate storage, or divide and conquer by splitting your rack into two collegiate-style racks for double the fun. As you can see, we put a lot of thought into this… our mom would be SO proud.

    More AWESOME features on the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack

    Hefty 11-Gauge Steel

    Built with 11-gauge steel, this bad boy can handle the weight capacity needs of world-class lifters. You get even more stability than our Light Commercial Rack which means less shaking when racking your bar or while using attachments. Even if it’s not bolted down, you’ll feel that true #heavyweight status.

    Laser Cut Numbers

    Trying to line up your j-cups perfectly can be a pain in the you-know-what. And unlike many of our competitors, we have lasered numbers every 1-2 holes to make adjustments a breeze. So you can easily change your j-cup height after your widow-maker squat set… because counting is HARD WORK when you can barely breathe.

    1" Spacing in Bench Zone

    Ready to settle for 2″ hole spacing throughout? Pffft, not a chance. You are an efficient and optimized MACHINE, that’s why we went with extra precise 1″ hole spacing to make unracking a heavy bar a little easier. After all, every morsel of energy matters when going for that 1lb PR (fractional plates, you da real MVP).

    No floor bolts required

    Flat foot racks have the distinct advantage of not having to anchor to the floor, simply attach the flat foot and voila, you are ready to set PR’s.

    Our flat foot supports come in 56″ and 62″ lengths, with an optional 43″ flat foot crossmember to connect them for added stability.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    The uprights on the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack measure true 3″ x 3″, which is exactly 76.2mm x 76.2mm with 5/8″ holes.

    Once assembled, the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack’s dimensions are:
    • 56” or 62″ length (depth)
    • 49” width
    • 74-1/4″, 86-1/4″, or 92-1/4″ height

    The Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack weighs a minimum of 250lbs when the shortest uprights & cross-pieces are selected with no attachments.

    The Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack’s weight capacity is 1,000lbs.

    The steel thickness on the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack is 11-gauge/2.9mm.

    Only attachments designed to fit true 3″ x 3″ uprights are guaranteed to fit on the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack. Attachments for uprights that measure 75mm x 75mm (often labeled as 3″ x 3″ but are based on the metric system and are actually 2.95″ x 2.95″) will NOT fit on the Hydra rack.

    To assemble the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack, you need two 24mm wrenches (not included by default). Correctly sized box-end, adjustable & socket wrenches are suitable alternatives.

    Covered under our Limited Lifetime Warranty guarantees that the Hydra 3 x 3 Inch Flat Foot Power Rack will not break under normal use, and protects against defects in material, function and workmanship.
